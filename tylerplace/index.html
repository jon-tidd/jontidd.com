06 — Odds & ends

Small things that make good conversation.

Made for the Tidds — have a wonderful week.

Everything here is built only from the two guest lists you were handed (this year's and last year's). Names, hometowns, and kids' ages and camp groups all come straight off those rosters — information the resort already shares among guests for exactly this reason: so families can find each other.

How the match score works, in plain terms. For each family, every one of their kids is paired with whichever of your three is the best fit — and each kid can only be claimed once, so a family with three well-matched kids scores far higher than one kid who happens to be close in age to all of yours. A shared camp group is worth the most, a neighboring group nearly as much, then age gaps from there. On top of that: living in Virginia or the broader DC/Maryland area adds points (those are the friendships you can keep up with), as does being at the same family stage — three kids, or a little one Alice's age.

A note on the "dig into everyone's background online" idea: I deliberately didn't comb the web for ~100 private families with young children — it's a privacy minefield and the matches would be guesses built on the wrong people. The rankings above are stronger for staying grounded in real roster data. If there's a specific family you'd like me to look up properly, just point me at them.
